SUNDAE SYSTEMS

CREATED BY VICTORIA ILIANA ANANYA EVE

We made this game because millions of people go to the grocery store and buy food without thinking about where it comes from. Every product has a backstory, and we want to show the systems and potential dangers involved in their creation. We used an ice-cream sundae to represent our system.There are consequences to your convenience. Be aware of the systems you are supporting.

CHOCOLATE/SUGAR

Our chocolate collection maze game was inspired by cocoa harvesting. Cocoa harvesting is possible through forced child labor and slavery. The maze reflects the difficulty of living under intensive labor, constantly running into barriers just to collect cocoa beans. You will only win after collecting the amount of beans you need for the day. This mini-game barely covers a small portion of the system in the production of chocolate.
There are no machines for harvesting cocoa, therefore people have to work long hot days picking the beans.

 

High demand for cocoa and a need for low production costs, leads to child labor. Children are recruited through trafficking and are forced to work long hours often in danger of chemical and physical harm.

 

As a consequence of being out in direct sunlight, workers frequently get heat exhaustion and develop skin cancer/abrasions. They frequently get cuts and injuries from dangerous tools, and poisoned from the pesticides.

 

Sugar cane workers in Central America work five hour shifts in the sun with no breaks, frequently working double shifts to support their families. Exhaustion, dehydration and long exposure to sun lead to kidney failure and death.

CHERRIES/SPRINKLES

Cherries and sprinkles are represented from our red-food dye related game. In this game you collect cochineal bugs as fast as you can before the time runs out. Through this fun mini-game we are trying to show how not only are these products derived from bugs, but how laborers are working quickly to avoid the heat. This game only represents a small portion of the system it takes in order to produce red-food dye found in many foods on the market. After the bugs are collected on cactus farms, they are transported to a lab where only then they are processed into dye. These farms are only located in specific regions of the world. To satisfy your food’s aesthetic, a long journey is taken.
The red dye used in making maraschino cherries and pink sprinkles comes from cochineal bugs.

 

70,000 insects are needed to produce a pound of dye. These bugs can only be harvested from hot climates.

 

The dye is listed as a ‘natural flavor’, but to many is a cause of severe allergic reactions.

DAIRY

Our farm game shows the process of producing the milk for ice cream and whipped cream. You start off with land, then grow food for the cows, corn. To simulate the real life consequences of a farmer growing crops, we developed a random risk for the corn crops to receive a bug infestation, preventing you from harvesting. If you acquire enough money, you can purchase a GMO upgrade for the corn. This means you can sell the corn for more money, but just like the farmers you run into an ethical dilemma. Although the long term health risks for GMO’s are not really known, GMO’s are being used to speed up the farming process. After harvesting enough corn, you can buy a cow. Then, you can collect milk from the cow. After you collect enough milk you win the game. There is much more in the process beyond what we put in the game. After the collection of the milk, it needs to be stored, shipped, and processed before the final phase can begin, which turns it into the final dairy product.
In 2008, more than 9.3 million cows were used to produce milk in the United States.

 

1,000 liters of water is required to produce just one liter of milk.

 

One cow can emit 100-200 liters of methane a day. Methane is 25 times more powerful than carbon dioxide as a greenhouse gas. Manure also contaminates drinking water by seeping into rivers, streams and the ground.

 

Only days or hours after birth, dairy cows are separated from their mothers. The mothers are milked until they can’t produce any more. Only after a few months, these same mother cows are artificially impregnated for their milk. As a result these cows suffer high levels of painful swelling in the glands and udder.They live this life for about five years before they are sent to slaughter.
